TITLE: Installing a Recessed Metal Back Box in Blockwork

OVERVIEW:
A recessed metal back box is installed when an accessory needs to sit flush in block or brickwork. This guide covers marking out, cutting the recess, installing oval trunking and securing the box neatly.

TOOLS / PPE:
- Hammer
- Chisel
- Screwdriver
- Level
- Pencil
- Safety glasses, boots, dust mask

MATERIALS:
- Metal back box
- Oval trunking
- PVC singles or suitable cable
- Grommets
- Fixings

SAFETY CHECKS:
- Wear eye protection when chiselling.
- Secure the block to prevent movement.
- Ensure any existing cables are isolated.

METHOD:
1. Mark the height and outline of the box using a level and pencil.
2. Score the outline with the chisel to create a clean edge.
3. Chisel out the material to the depth of the box, keeping edges square.
4. Knock out an entry hole and fit a grommet.
5. Install oval trunking into the recess and secure it.
6. Place the box flush and level; secure it with screws.
7. Pull cables through and leave suitable tails.
8. Clean the work area.

GOOD PRACTICE:
- Box sits flush and level.
- Cables protected with grommets.
- Trunking fixed in straight, neat lines.
