TITLE: Installing a Drylining Back Box in Plasterboard

OVERVIEW:
Drylining boxes are used for mounting sockets or switches in plasterboard. This guide covers cutting the opening and securing the box neatly.

TOOLS / PPE:
- Pad saw
- Screwdriver
- Level
- Pencil
- Measuring tape
- Safety glasses

MATERIALS:
- Drylining box (single or double)
- T&E cable or singles

SAFETY CHECKS:
- Ensure no cables or pipes are behind the plasterboard.
- Wear eye protection when cutting.

METHOD:
1. Mark the box outline at the correct height using a level.
2. Drill a small starter hole.
3. Cut out the shape using a pad saw.
4. Pull the cable into the opening.
5. Insert the drylining box and tighten the lugs until secure.
6. Check the box is level and flush.

GOOD PRACTICE:
- Do not overtighten lugs.
- Cut cleanly to avoid gaps.
