Basic electricity:
• Voltage (V), Current (I), Resistance (R), Power (P).
• Ohm’s Law: V = I × R.
• Power formulas: P = V × I, P = I²R, P = V² / R.

Series circuits:
• Current same everywhere.
• Voltages share.
• Resistances add: Rt = R1+R2+R3.

Parallel circuits:
• Voltage same on each branch.
• Currents share.
• Formula: 1/Rt = 1/R1+1/R2+1/R3.

AC supply:
• UK: 230V ±10%, 50 Hz.
• Single-phase vs three-phase.

Earthing:
• Provides fault path.
• Enables ADS (automatic disconnection of supply).

Magnetism:
• Coils produce magnetic fields.
• Transformers use induction:
  Vs/Vp = Ns/Np.

Generation:
• Power stations → transformers → transmission → distribution.

RCD operation:
• Trip when imbalance in L & N.
• Common rating: 30 mA.

Units:
• V, A, Ω, W, kW, kWh.
